GENERAL ONLINE SAFETY


1. USE STRONG, UNIQUE PASSWORDS
     Create passwords with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and
     symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple accounts.
     Use a password manager (e.g., Bitwarden) to keep track of them.As passwords tend to get leaked from databases often, try being aware of those breaches.

2. ENABLE TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (2FA)
     Turn on 2FA for all important accounts — email, social media, and
     college portals. This adds a second layer of protection even if
     your password is stolen. Try enabling 2fa for all accounts if available and don't resort for just the important ones.

3. AVOID PUBLIC WI-FI FOR SENSITIVE TASKS
     Do not access your bank account, college login, or personal data
     on unsecured public Wi-Fi (cafes, hostels, bus stands). Use your
     mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.

Be aware of even entering the wifi credentials as that could be pathway to entry.

4. VERIFY BEFORE YOU CLICK
     Do not click on suspicious links received via WhatsApp, SMS, or
     email — even if they appear to come from known contacts. Phishing
     links often mimic trusted brands or government portals.

Verify before you access a website too as generative AI has made it easier to dupe a legitimate website and lure you into entering your credentials.

5. KEEP APPS AND DEVICES UPDATED
     Regularly update your phone's OS, apps, and antivirus software.
     Updates patch security vulnerabilities that hackers actively exploit.

And also beware of those updates as certain update can break or fail your system, so make sure to keep backups as well in a secure platform or source.

6. PROTECT YOUR AADHAAR AND PERSONAL IDs
     Never share photos of your Aadhaar card, PAN card, or college ID
in WhatsApp groups or unknown websites. Use the masked version available at uidai.gov.in if the need is there.

And enable 2 factor for these as well, With your phone number and digital signatures

FINANCIAL & UPI SAFETY


7. NEVER SHARE YOUR UPI PIN, OTP, OR BANK DETAILS WITH ANYONE
     No bank, UPI apps (GPay, Paytm, PhonePe), or government agencies
     would ask for your UPI PIN or OTP over calls or messages.
     UPI PIN is like your ATM PIN; keep it private.

8. BEWARE OF "RECEIVE MONEY" SCAMS ON UPI
     Scammers send UPI payment requests to trick people.
     Remember, entering your UPI PIN on the payment request SENDING
     money is what the request is for; it DOES NOT receive the money.
     Enter the PIN carefully before sending the money.

SOCIAL MEDIA SAFETY


9. AUDIT YOUR SOCIAL MEDIA PRIVACY SETTINGS
     Ensure your Instagram, Facebook, and Snapchat profiles are set to private.
     Don’t publicly disclose your college name, hostel location, or daily routine,
     as this could be misused.

10. DO NOT ACCEPT UNKNOWN FRIEND/FOLLOW REQUESTS
     Fake profiles are created to get your personal info or trick college
     students into romance/scam investments. Verify before accepting.
