#Social Media Checklist for Responsible Use –
 
DO'S

1. Think before you post – consider whether you would be comfortable with what you are about to post with your professor, employer, or your family members viewing it.
2. Update your privacy settings – regularly review who can see your posts, your profile, and your personal information.
3. Give credit where credit is due – always give credit to the creators of work or ideas you are reposting.
4. Use social media to learn and grow – use it to learn from credible sources, industry leaders, and academic communities in your field of interest.
5. Be respectful in your online interactions – engage with opposing views in a respectful manner, even if you don't agree with them.

Social Media Checklist for Responsible Use – 

DON'TS

1. Do not share your personal information – refrain from publicly posting your address, phone number, your daily routine, your current location, etc.
2. Do not spread unverified information – always verify news, statistics, or information you intend to share to avoid spreading false information.
3. Do not use social media on impulse, especially if you are feeling emotional – refrain from using it to vent your anger, frustrations, etc., because once posted, you cannot undo what you have posted.
4. Do not allow yourself to get bullied – neither participate in nor condone cyberbullying.
5. Do not allow your academic focus to get derailed by your use of social media – don't use it in your classes, for your homework, or for your exams.
